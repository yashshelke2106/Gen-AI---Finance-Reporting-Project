## Revenue Drivers

- ▲ **Subscription segment** — Growth driven by net new logo additions and expanded seats within existing accounts.
- ▬ **Professional services revenue** — Professional services revenue remained roughly flat year-over-year.

## Margin Changes

- **Gross margin:** 44.0% (+300 bps). Improved due to cloud infrastructure cost optimization and modest price increases.
- **Operating margin:** 24.0% (—). Improved as revenue grew faster than operating expenses.

## Risk Flags

- **[HIGH] Customer concentration** — The company's three largest customers account for a significant portion of revenue, posing a material risk if any are lost. Management is actively diversifying the customer base.
  > _Our top three customers remain a meaningful portion of revenue. We are actively diversifying the base, and net new logo growth this quarter is a direct result of that effort._
- **[MEDIUM] Foreign currency exchange rate fluctuations** — An increasing share of revenue is generated internationally, exposing the company to foreign currency exchange rate fluctuations.
  > _International is our fastest-growing region, which does increase our sensitivity to currency movements. We manage that exposure actively._

## Management Tone / Sentiment Shift

**More Positive** (confidence 90%)
- raising our full-year revenue guidance
- We feel more confident today than we did a quarter ago about our ability to execute.

## Plain-English Summary

Acme Corp delivered a strong second quarter, with revenue increasing by 13.6% year-over-year, primarily driven by its subscription business. The company also improved its gross margin to 44.0% due to cost optimization and strategic pricing. This positive performance led management to raise its full-year revenue guidance, indicating confidence in continued growth and profitability. However, the company faces risks related to customer concentration and foreign currency fluctuations.
